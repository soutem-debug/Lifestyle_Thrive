Wild Swimming in Yorkshire

Posted by Emma on 13/04/2021 Hobbies

The Introduction

Believe or not wild swimming can have untold benefits for your heath, mental well-being and overall happiness. Wild swimming is proven to boost your mood, reduce stress and be meditative. A long with the many significant benefits for your mind and well-being, it is proven to reduce inflammatory conditions, aches, pains, lower blood pressure and help type 2 diabetes.

So why haven’t you been taking the plunge? Just don’t know where to go? The good news is there are many beautiful spots to enjoy and you probably don’t have to travel as far as you think.

Bloggers top tips:

Eel Pool Description: Correctly known as the Long Eel, this is a big pool on the river Ribble just downstream from the popular Stainforth Foss and a regular Dales Dippers haunt. A good 50 metres of swimmable distance and up to 4 metres deep in places. Rocks at the side for changing and jumping/diving off. Access not good for the mobility impaired (see notes below on slippery and steep access).

Janets Foss Description: - A delightful small pool in woodland near Malham, one of the Dales Dipper’s favourites and used in winter for our floodlit swims. VERY popular with visitors so does get extremely busy throughout the day and early evening. Early morning is best to have a peaceful dip here. Parking and directions: - Janets’ Foss is located at - https://w3w.co/tangible.snack.contained
