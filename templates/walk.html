am sure it comes as no shock that walking/hiking has great perks when it comes to health and well-ness as well as nice views, fresh air, and the sounds and smells of nature.

Hiking is a powerful cardio workout that can:

  • Lower your risk of heart disease
  • Improve your blood pressure and blood sugar levels
  • Strengthen your core and improve balance

Bloggers top tips

Malham Cove and Tarn Distance: 13km Duration: 3-4 hours Difficulty: Easy-moderate

Malham was the first hike I did when I first moved to Yorkshire. It has a breath-taking limestone cove which is a steep climb but when you are stood at the top, allows you to see the Yorkshire Dales topography at its finest. Along with the stunning views it has an intricate web of streams which you can paddle in, the water is clear and on a still day serene.

Burnsall-Grassington Distance: 13km Duration: 3 hours Difficulty: Easy

This riverside walk follows the River Wharfe around the stunning countryside around the villages of Burnstall and Grassington. Both villages are market towns meaning there is also something weird and wonderful to discover. It is an easy walk making it one of the most tranquil hikes in Yorkshire.
